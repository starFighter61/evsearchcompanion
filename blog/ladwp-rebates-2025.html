LADWP EV Charger Rebates 2025: Complete Step-by-Step Guide

Everything West LA residents need to know about getting $1,000 for home EV charger installation. Application process, eligibility requirements, and insider tips included.

Get Up To $1,000 Back

Key Takeaways

  • $1,000 rebate available for LADWP customers installing Level 2 EV chargers
  • Application must be submitted BEFORE installation - this is crucial
  • Processing time: 6-8 weeks after final inspection
  • Compatible with California state rebates - stack your savings!

What is the LADWP EV Charger Rebate?

Los Angeles Department of Water and Power (LADWP) offers a $1,000 rebate to residential customers who install qualified Level 2 electric vehicle charging equipment at their homes. This program is designed to encourage EV adoption and support the city's clean energy goals.

Program Highlights

Rebate Amount

Up to $1,000

Eligibility

LADWP residential customers

Processing Time

6-8 weeks after approval

Stackable

Yes, with state rebates

Am I Eligible?

To qualify for the LADWP EV charger rebate, you must meet these requirements:

1

Active LADWP Account

You must be an LADWP residential electric customer with an active account in good standing.

2

Own or Lease an EV

You must own or lease a qualified plug-in electric vehicle (BEV or PHEV).

3

Qualified Charger

Install a new Level 2 (240V) charging station at your primary residence. Must be ENERGY STAR certified or equivalent.

4

Licensed Electrician

Installation must be completed by a California licensed electrician with proper permits.

Important: Application must be submitted BEFORE you install the charger or begin any work. Post-installation applications are not accepted.

Step-by-Step Application Process

Follow these steps carefully to ensure your rebate application is approved:

Step 1: Get Pre-Approval

Before purchasing equipment or hiring an electrician:

  • Visit the LADWP rebate portal at ladwp.com/ev
  • Create an account or log in with your LADWP account number
  • Complete the online pre-approval application
  • Upload proof of EV ownership (registration or lease agreement)
  • Wait for pre-approval confirmation (usually 3-5 business days)

Step 2: Choose Your Equipment

Select a qualified Level 2 charger from the approved equipment list. Popular options for West LA homes include:

  • ChargePoint Home Flex - WiFi-enabled, $699
  • JuiceBox 40 - Smart charging, $599
  • Grizzl-E - Budget-friendly, $399
  • Tesla Wall Connector - For Tesla owners, $475

ðŸ’¡ Tip: Choose a charger with smart features to take advantage of LADWP's EV-friendly time-of-use rates.

Step 3: Hire a Licensed Electrician

Find a California licensed electrician to install your charger. Make sure they:

  • Pull the required electrical permit from LA City
  • Provide a detailed installation quote
  • Are familiar with LADWP rebate requirements
  • Schedule final inspection with the city

Average installation cost in West LA: $800 - $2,000 depending on distance from panel and any needed electrical upgrades.

Step 4: Complete Installation

Once you have pre-approval:

  • Electrician installs the charger and completes work
  • Schedule city inspection (required)
  • Obtain "final inspection" approval from LA City
  • Test the charger to ensure it's working properly

Step 5: Submit Final Application

Within 90 days of installation, submit your final rebate application with:

  • Proof of payment (itemized invoice from electrician)
  • Receipt for EV charger purchase
  • Copy of final electrical inspection approval
  • Photos of installed charger showing serial number
  • Proof of EV ownership/registration

Step 6: Receive Your Rebate

After LADWP reviews your application:

  • Processing time: 6-8 weeks
  • Rebate issued via check or direct deposit
  • You'll receive confirmation email once processed

Insider Tips for Faster Approval

  • Apply Early

    LADWP rebates are first-come, first-served. Submit pre-approval ASAP to reserve your spot.

  • Use a Certified Installer

    Some electricians are LADWP-certified and familiar with documentation requirements.

  • Keep Everything Digital

    Scan all receipts, permits, and photos immediately. Upload high-quality PDFs.

  • Take Detailed Photos

    Include serial numbers, electrical panel, final installation. More photos = fewer questions.

  • Stack with State Rebates

    Apply for California Clean Vehicle Rebate at the same time. They don't conflict.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Installing before pre-approval: This is the #1 reason applications get denied. Always get pre-approval first!

Skipping the city inspection: Final inspection approval is mandatory. No inspection = no rebate.

Missing the 90-day deadline: Submit your final application within 90 days of installation completion.

Incomplete documentation: Missing receipts or poor-quality photos delay processing.

FAQs

Can I apply if I live in an apartment?

Yes, but you'll need landlord approval and proper documentation. The charger must be installed at your primary residence with LADWP service.

What if I already installed a charger?

Unfortunately, you're not eligible. The rebate requires pre-approval before installation begins. This policy is strictly enforced.

Do I need a separate meter for my EV charger?

No, a separate meter is not required for the rebate. However, you may want to enroll in LADWP's EV time-of-use rate plan to save on charging costs.

How long does the whole process take?

From pre-approval to receiving your rebate check: typically 8-12 weeks total. Pre-approval takes 3-5 days, installation 1-3 weeks, and rebate processing 6-8 weeks.
